"Good morning, class. How was your weekend?"

"It was great! I went on a hiking trip with my friends."

"That sounds wonderful. Did you see any wildlife?"

"Yes, we saw some deer and even a bear. It was so thrilling!"

"Wow, that's quite an adventure. Did you take any photos?"

"Yeah, I did. I'll show you after class."

"Sounds great. Today, we will be learning about idioms. Do any of you know what an idiom is?"

"Yeah, it's a saying that doesn't mean what the individual words say."

"Exactly. Can you give me an example?"

"Sure. 'Break a leg' doesn't actually mean to break your leg. It means good luck."

"Very good! Now let's try another one. 'Pulling someone's leg'. Any ideas?"

"It means teasing or joking with someone. My dad is always pulling my leg."

"Right on the money. You guys are doing great. Let's move on to the next one."

"Can we learn some more idioms? This is fun!"

"Sure thing. Let's keep going!"
