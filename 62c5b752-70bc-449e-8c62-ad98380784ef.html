Good morning, class! Let's start today's lesson by discussing idioms. Can anyone give me an example of an idiom that you've heard before?

How about "raining cats and dogs"? I've heard that one a lot.

Great job, B! That's a perfect example of an idiom. Can you tell me what it means?

It means it's raining really hard outside.

Correct! Now, does anyone know the origin of that idiom?

Um, well, I'm not quite sure.

It actually dates back to the 17th century, when people living in cities would throw their waste out of the windows onto the streets. During heavy rainfalls, the streets would flood and carry the waste, including dead animals, so it would look like it was "raining cats and dogs."

Wow, that's really interesting! I never would've guessed that's where it came from.
