Hey there! I'm a fisherman here to check out the local seafood at the Tsukiji fish market, mind showing me around?

Welcome and sure thing! As a guide, it's my job to help people pick out the freshest and best quality seafood in the market.

Perfect, I'm most interested in the fish. What should I look out for when selecting them?

Well, for starters, you want to make sure the eyes of the fish are clear, not cloudy. This indicates freshness. Also, pay attention to the gills, they should be bright red.

Got it, so if the fish looks dull and the gills are brownish, it's not the best choice?

Correct. You also want to check the texture of the fish's skin. It should be smooth and not slimy.

That all sounds helpful! What about shellfish?

With shellfish, freshness is key. Look for closed and tightly shut shells, and avoid those with broken shells or bad odors.

I see. What can you tell me about sushi-grade tuna?

Sushi-grade tuna is of premium quality, and often used in high-end restaurants. It should have a bright red flesh and firm texture.

Thanks! This has been so educational. Do you have any personal favorites in the market to recommend?

Personally, I love uni or sea urchin. It has a sweet, buttery taste. Plus, they're usually sold with their shells and spines for decoration!

Wow, I've never tried uni before. I'll definitely have to give that a try. Thanks for the recommendation!

No problem, happy to help! Don't forget to haggle for the best deals and enjoy your fresh seafood!
