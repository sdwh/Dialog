"Hey, Captain B! How do you think the future of fishing is going to look like?"

"Well, I think it's going to be more technology-driven. We're already seeing drones and underwater robots being used, and I bet there's more to come."

"That's true. But I also think we need to focus more on sustainability. We can't keep catching fish at the rate we are now, or else we'll run out."

"Right, that's a good point. Maybe we should start looking into farm-raising fish instead of only relying on wild ones."

"That could be a good solution. But it might take time for people to get used to it. Some people are still hesitant about eating farm-raised fish."

"Yeah, I see your point. But we can also educate them on the benefits and how it can help conserve our fish population."

"True. And I think another trend we'll see is more focus on niche markets. People are willing to pay more for sustainably caught fish and exotic varieties."

"That's a possibility. It could also mean smaller boats and crews, but a higher price per pound."

"Exactly. And who knows, maybe there will be new species discovered in the future that we can target."

"Ha! That would be amazing. Imagine catching a new type of fish and being the first to bring it to market."

"Almost sounds like a dream come true, doesn't it?"

"It does indeed. But overall, I think the future of fishing will require us to adapt and be creative."

"Agreed. Change can be difficult, but I think we have the skills and knowledge to make it work."
