Brushes can make a huge difference! For blush, you should use a fluffy brush and apply in a circular motion on the apples of your cheeks. For bronzer, a larger angled brush can help you distribute the product evenly on your cheekbones.

Thanks, that's super helpful! I'm also curious about lip products. What's the difference between lip gloss and lipstick, and which one should I use for a more natural look?

Lip gloss is shiny and provides some moisture, while lipstick is usually more opaque and comes in a wider range of colors. If you're going for a natural look, I'd recommend a tinted lip balm or a cream lipstick in a nude shade.

Ah, got it. One last question - how do you make lipstick last longer? I feel like mine always fades after a couple of hours.

A lip liner can help define your lips and create a barrier to keep lipstick in place. You can also try blotting your lipstick with a tissue after you apply it, and then reapplying a second layer for a more long-lasting effect.
