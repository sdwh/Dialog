Good morning class! How was everyone’s weekend?

It was great, thanks for asking!

Glad to hear that. Now, let’s get to our lesson on English idioms. Who can give me an example of an idiom?

How about “the early bird catches the worm”?

Excellent example! Now, can you give me the meaning of that idiom?

It means that those who are quick to act will have an advantage.

That’s correct. How about this one: “break a leg”?

Isn’t that like wishing someone good luck?

Yes, it’s a way to wish someone good luck before a performance. But why “break a leg”?

Hmm, I’m not sure.

Well, it’s because in the theatre world, saying “good luck” is considered bad luck. So instead, they say the opposite.

Ah, I see.

Okay, let’s move on to some funny idioms. How about “it’s raining cats and dogs”?

Does that mean it’s really raining hard?

Yes, exactly. But can you imagine if it actually rained cats and dogs?

(laughs) That would be crazy!

Indeed it would. Alright, let’s end the lesson there for today. Remember to use these idioms in your everyday conversations to impress your friends and family.

Thank you, teacher! I definitely learned something new today.
