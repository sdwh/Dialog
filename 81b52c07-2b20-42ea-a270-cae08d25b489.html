Good morning, B. Thank you for giving us the opportunity to interview you today. How are you feeling?

It’s my pleasure, A. I’m feeling great. The weather is perfect for our interview, too.

Yes, it’s a beautiful day. So, can you tell us a little about your background and how you got into your current profession?

Certainly. I actually started out as a clown in a circus. But after a while, I wanted to do something a bit more serious. That’s when I stumbled upon acting.

Wow, that’s quite a diverse career path. So, in terms of acting, what type of roles do you usually take on?

I actually enjoy playing villains. There’s just something about being able to let loose and play an evil character that’s so satisfying.

That’s interesting. Is it hard to switch from playing a villain to a more sympathetic character?

It can be a bit challenging, but that’s what makes acting so fun – the ability to step into different roles and challenging ourselves.

Definitely. So, I have to ask, what’s the most difficult scene you’ve ever had to film?

It was probably a scene where I had to run and scream at the same time. It sounds easy, but it was actually quite difficult to get the timing right.

I can imagine. So, aside from acting, what else do you enjoy doing in your free time?

I love playing board games with friends and family. There’s just something about the competitive nature that’s so exhilarating.

That’s great. Thank you so much for taking the time to talk with us today, B. It’s been a pleasure.

It’s been my pleasure as well, A. Thank you for having me.
