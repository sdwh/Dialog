Good afternoon, sir. Can you please state your name for the record?

Uh, sure. My name is John Smith.

Thank you, Mr. Smith. Now, can you tell us why you were seen entering the property that was reported to have been burglarized?

(Laughs nervously) Well, officer, I was actually looking for my dog. He ran away and I thought he might have gone into that house.

(Raises an eyebrow) Really? And why didn't you just knock on the door and ask the residents if they had seen him?

(Scratches his head) Well, I guess I didn't want to disturb them. Plus, my dog is pretty shy so he might have run off.

I see. And what's your dog's name?

(Smiling) His name is Max. He's a golden retriever.

(Smiling back) Ah, I see. Those are great dogs. Have you found him yet?

(Shaking his head) Not yet. But I'm still looking.

Well, Mr. Smith, we'll be looking further into this case, but I hope you don't mind if we keep an eye out for Max as well.

(Grinning) Of course not, officer. The more eyes looking for him, the better.

(Chuckling) All right, Mr. Smith. You're free to go for now, but please let us know if you do find your dog.

(Standing up) Thank you, officer. I will.

(Smiling) Have a good day, sir.

You too.
