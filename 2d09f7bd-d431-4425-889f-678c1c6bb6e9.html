Good evening! I would like to try your famous Swiss alcoholic drinks, what would you recommend?

Well, Absinthe is definitely a must-try, it's a classic Swiss spirit with a long history.

That sounds interesting! Can you tell me more about it?

Of course! Absinthe is known for its strong anise flavor and green color. It was a popular drink among artists and writers during the 19th century, and there were even rumors that it could cause hallucinations.

That sounds like quite an interesting history! How do you serve it?

We traditionally use a special Absinthe spoon to place a sugar cube on top of the Absinthe, and then pour cold water over it to create a milky appearance.

Oh, that looks really unique! What other Swiss spirits do you have?

Well, we also have Kirsch, which is a clear cherry brandy, and a variety of herbal liqueurs such as Appenzeller and Enzian.

Wow, I'm impressed by the variety here! What's your personal favorite?

I'm a big fan of Appenzeller, which is a spicy herbal liqueur made with local alpine herbs. It's great as a digestif after a heavy meal.

That sounds enticing! I'll have to try it before leaving. Do you have any food pairings that go well with your drinks?

Definitely! I suggest pairing your Absinthe with some cheese or a savory snack, and the Appenzeller goes really well with a charcuterie board or hearty meat dishes.
