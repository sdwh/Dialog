Hey B, have you ever heard of ISO 27001 disaster recovery?

ISO what now? Sounds like a mouthful.

It’s actually a set of guidelines for managing information security risks, including how to handle disasters and recover important data.

Ahh, got it. So why should I care about it?

Well, if our company were to experience a disaster, like a flood or cyber attack, we need to have a plan in place to ensure we can recover our data and systems as quickly as possible.

That makes sense. So what are the key things we need to know?

First, we need to conduct a risk assessment to identify all potential threats, from natural disasters to human error. Then, we need to develop a response plan to mitigate those risks and to ensure business continuity.

Okay, I’m with you so far. How do we actually implement that plan?

That’s where things get a bit trickier. We need to ensure we have offsite backups of all important data, and that we have a way to access that data in the event of a disaster. We also need to test our plan regularly to ensure it works as intended.

Good to know. So what happens if we don’t have a solid disaster recovery plan in place?

It could lead to significant downtime for our company, which could impact our revenue and reputation. Plus, it could lead to data loss, which is never a good thing.

Yikes, that sounds like a nightmare. I’m glad we’re taking this seriously.

Absolutely. It’s always better to be prepared than to be caught off guard in a crisis.

Thanks for walking me through this. Can’t wait to see how we can implement it at our company.

No problem, happy to help. Let’s make sure we’re ready for anything that comes our way!
