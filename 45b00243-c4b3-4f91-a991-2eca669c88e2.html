Good morning, class! We have a fun topic today: superheroes. Who's your favorite superhero?

I like Iron Man. He's smart and has a lot of cool gadgets.

Ah, good choice. Did you know that Iron Man's suit is actually inspired by a real-life invention called exoskeleton?

Really? That's cool! Can I make my own exoskeleton suit?

Haha, maybe someday in the future. But for now, let's focus on today's lesson. Did you guys know that Superman was originally created to be a villain?

No way! Why would they do that?

I guess they thought it would be interesting to have a character with immense power but uses it for evil. But as the story developed, they realized that Superman's true potential was being a hero.

That's so cool! I'm glad they changed their minds.

Me too! Now, let's do a group activity. I'm going to assign each of you a superhero and I want you to come up with a creative way on how they can use their powers.

Awesome! Can't wait to get started.

Great. And don't forget, even though they may have superpowers, it's their heroism that truly makes them a hero.

Duly noted, teacher. Let's get to work!
