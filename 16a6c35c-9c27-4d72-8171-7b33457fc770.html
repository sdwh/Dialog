Good morning, class! Today, we are going to talk about idioms. Can someone give me an example of an idiom they know?

How about "raining cats and dogs?" It means it's raining heavily.

Great example! Now, can you think of one that hasn't been mentioned yet?

Hmm, how about "let the cat out of the bag?" It means to reveal a secret.

Excellent! You're on a roll. Can you use that idiom in a sentence?

Sure! "I accidentally let the cat out of the bag about the surprise party."

Very good. Now, can you make up an idiom of your own?

How about "hit the pillow?" It means to go to sleep.

Nice one! You've got some creativity there. Okay, class, let's try to use these idioms in a conversation. Student A, why don't you start?

"It's raining cats and dogs outside. I hope it stops by the time we have to leave."

"Me too. I need to let the cat out of the bag to my friend about her surprise party tonight."

"Be careful not to spill the beans! Remember, it's a surprise. Anyone feeling sleepy from the rain can hit the pillow right after class."

"Sounds good to me. I could use a nap."

"Me too. Thanks for the lesson, Teacher A."
