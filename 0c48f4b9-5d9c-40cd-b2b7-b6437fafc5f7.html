Hey B, have you heard of ISO 27001 Social Engineering? It’s a really interesting topic.

No, I haven’t. What’s it about?

Basically, it’s the practice of using psychologically manipulative techniques to gain unauthorized access to sensitive information.

Oh, that sounds like something out of a spy movie. Is it really that effective?

Unfortunately, it is. Social engineering has become a popular tactic for hackers because it’s much easier to manipulate people than to bypass security systems.

That’s scary. So, what can we do to protect ourselves against it?

The key is education and awareness. Employees need to be trained to recognize common social engineering techniques, such as phishing emails and phone scams.

Makes sense. Do you know of any real-life examples of social engineering attacks?

One infamous example is the “Nigerian prince” email scam. Hackers sent out emails claiming to be Nigerian royalty looking to give away millions, but in reality, it was just a ploy to steal people’s personal information.

Ha! I actually received one of those emails once, but I didn’t fall for it.

Good for you! It’s easy to fall prey to these attacks, but with the right education and awareness, we can protect ourselves and our organizations from data breaches.

Absolutely. It’s fascinating how much psychology plays a role in something as technical as cyber security.

Definitely! It’s a reminder that even the most advanced security measures can be easily defeated by human error.

Well, I’m going to make sure to educate myself and my team about ISO 27001 Social Engineering. Thanks for the heads up, A.
