Good morning, class! Today, we’re going to dive into the most exciting part of English grammar.

Really? What could be more exciting than grammar?

Punctuation, of course! It’s like the seasoning that adds flavor to your writing.

Hmm, I never thought about it that way. So, what are we going to learn first?

Let’s start with the comma. Can you give me an example where a comma can change the entire meaning of a sentence?

Uh, how about “Let’s eat, Grandma” versus “Let’s eat Grandma”?

You got it! Now, let’s try this exercise. Add the necessary punctuation marks to this sentence: “A woman without her man is nothing.”

(writes) “A woman, without her, man is nothing.”

Good job! Now, let’s move on to apostrophes. They can be used to show possession or contraction. Can you give me an example of each?

Sure, “That’s mine” is a contraction and “That’s John’s book” is showing possession.

Impressive! Now, let’s end this lesson with a fun game. I’ll give you a sentence and you have to identify all the punctuation marks in it. Are you ready?

Bring it on!

Excellent job, B! I think you’re ready to become a punctuation master.
