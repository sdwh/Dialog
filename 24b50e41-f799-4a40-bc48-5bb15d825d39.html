Hi B, thanks for meeting me here. It's been a while since we've caught up.

No problem, A. How have you been?

Busy as usual, working on a new book idea. Actually, that's what I wanted to discuss with you.

Okay, I'm all ears. What's the idea?

Well, it's a romance novel set in the 1920s. Think flapper dresses, jazz music, and prohibition-era speakeasies.

Sounds interesting. What's the target audience?

I'm thinking it would appeal to both younger and older readers who enjoy historical fiction with a romantic twist.

That's a broad audience. Can you give me a brief synopsis?

Sure. It's about a young woman who falls in love with a jazz musician but struggles to balance her passion with the social expectations of the time.

I like it. Do you have a title in mind?

Tentatively, it's called "Star-Crossed Swing".

Oh, I like it. Have you started writing it yet?

Yes, I'm about a quarter of the way through.

Great. Do you have a deadline in mind?

I'm hoping to finish the manuscript in about 6 months.

Okay, we can work with that. I'll start looking into publishers who would be a good fit for this book.

Thanks, B. You always know what to do.

It's my job, A. But I'm excited to see how this book turns out.

Me too. It's definitely a departure from my usual fare, so I'm nervous but also excited.

That's a good sign. It means you're pushing yourself as a writer.

I hope so. But for now, how about we order a cup of coffee and catch up some more?

Sounds like a plan. Let's do that.
