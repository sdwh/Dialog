Hey B, have you ever heard of ISO 27001 Security Assessment?

Hmm, I can’t say I have. What is it?

It’s a process to evaluate and check if an organization has proper security measures in place to protect its information assets.

Ah, got it. So, it’s like a security check or audit for companies?

Exactly! There’s a list of controls that need to be in place for certification.

Do you know what kind of controls are included?

Yep! Some of them are access control, information security policies, risk assessment, and incident management.

Wow, that sounds like a long list. How does the assessment process work?

Well, there are two stages. First, a preliminary review is done to assess the readiness of the organization. Then, a full assessment takes place, including document reviews and interviews with employees.

I see. Is this assessment internationally recognized?

Yes, it is! That’s why it’s called ISO – it’s recognized worldwide.

Interesting. Is it difficult to get the certification?

It can be a long and complicated process to get everything in order, but it’s definitely worth it for the certification.

I see your point, but do you think it’s really necessary for every company?

It really depends on the type of company and the importance of its information assets, but it’s certainly a good practice to have proper security measures in place regardless.

Gotcha. It’s definitely something to keep in mind for the future.

Definitely! We should start implementing some of these controls in our organization.

Agreed! Let's start with information security policies.

Great idea! And we can prioritize the other controls from there.

Yes, and soon we can get our ISO 27001 Security Certification!

That’d be awesome! Let’s get started on this security journey.
