Dad, I had a cold last week and I'm feeling much better now.

That's great to hear, kiddo. Do you know what caused your cold?

I'm not sure, but some of my friends think it's because of the weather.

It's definitely possible. Changes in temperature and humidity can weaken our immune system, making it easier to catch a cold.

Does that mean if it's hot outside, we won't get sick?

Not necessarily. Even if it's hot, we can still catch a cold if we come into contact with someone who has it.

What about fever? Is that also caused by the weather?

A fever is actually a symptom of an underlying condition, like an infection. However, weather changes can make it easier for us to catch these infections.

Oh, I see. So, what can we do to prevent getting sick?

We should always practice good hygiene, like washing our hands frequently and avoiding touching our face. We should also try to maintain a healthy lifestyle with a balanced diet and regular exercise.

That makes sense. Thanks Dad!

No problem, kiddo. Just remember to take care of yourself, especially during the change of seasons.
