Good morning class! How are you all feeling today?

Good morning sir! We're feeling great and ready to learn.

That's great to hear. Today we'll be talking about idioms. Do you know what an idiom is?

Yes sir, it's a phrase that doesn't mean exactly what it says.

Fantastic! Can you give me an example?

Sure sir, "it's raining cats and dogs" means it's raining heavily.

Excellent work! Now, who knows the meaning of "break a leg"?

I do sir! It means good luck.

Right you are! Do any of you know the origin of that phrase?

I think it comes from theatre, sir. Actors used to say it to each other before going on stage.

Very good, you're absolutely right! Now, let's make our own idioms. Who has an idea?

How about "spill the beans", sir?

Great choice, and what would that mean?

It would mean to reveal a secret.

Excellent! Let's try some more, class. How about "out of the blue"?

That means something happens unexpectedly, sir.

Spot on! I'm very impressed with your knowledge and creativity, class. Keep up the good work!
