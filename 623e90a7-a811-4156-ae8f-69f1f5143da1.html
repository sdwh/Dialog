Good morning class!

Good morning, sir!

Today we’re going to talk about idioms. Have you heard of them?

Yes, sir. They’re phrases that don’t mean what the words suggest.

Very good! Can you give me an example?

“Break a leg.” It doesn’t mean to actually break your leg, it means good luck.

Excellent! How about “when pigs fly”?

It means something is unlikely to happen.

Right! Now let me ask you a difficult one. What does “cat got your tongue” mean?

It means you’re speechless or have nothing to say.

You got it! Now, can you use “cat got your tongue” in a sentence?

I was so nervous during my presentation that I forgot everything. The cat got my tongue!

Haha, very good! Now, let’s move to the next lesson.

Sound good, sir! Thanks for teaching us about idioms. It's really a fun way to use language.

You're welcome, B! I'm glad that you enjoyed it. Remember, using idioms can make your English sound more natural and interesting.
