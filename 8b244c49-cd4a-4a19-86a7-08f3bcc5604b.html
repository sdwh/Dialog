Good morning, class. How’s everyone doing today?

Good morning, Professor. We’re doing great. How about you?

I’m doing fantastic. So, today we’re going to talk about some new vocabulary words. Who wants to start?

I can start, Professor. The first word is ‘serendipity.’ It means a fortunate accident.

Excellent choice, B. The next word is ‘onomatopoeia.’ Can anyone tell me what that means?

Onomatopoeia are words that sound similar to the noise they describe, right?

That’s right. Now, how about this one: ‘kerfuffle.’ Anyone familiar with this one?

I think it means a commotion or fuss.

Very good, B. That’s exactly what it means. Now, let's move on to some idioms. B, can you give us an example of an idiom?

Sure, Professor. ‘Break a leg’ is an idiom. It means good luck.

Absolutely correct. One more idiom for today: ‘raining cats and dogs.’ What does that mean?

It means it’s raining heavily.

Brilliant. Well done, B. That’s all for today. Keep up the excellent work, and see you next class!
