Have you seen those new wheelchair accessible hotels that have been popping up?

No, I haven't. What are they like?

They have wider doorways, lower beds, and roll-in showers. It's great for people with mobility issues.

That's amazing! Do they cost more than regular hotels?

Unfortunately, yes. But it's worth it for the convenience and accessibility.

I agree. It's important for everyone to have equal access to travel accommodations.

Absolutely. And it's not just hotels, but also restaurants, attractions, and public transportation that need to be accessible.

Yes, and it's not just for people with physical disabilities, but also for people with sensory and cognitive disabilities.

Exactly! It's not just about being physically unable to do something, but about accommodating different needs.

Accessibility is a journey, not a destination. We need to constantly strive to be more inclusive and thoughtful.
