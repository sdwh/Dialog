Welcome to Melbourne, B! Are you excited to explore the city?

Absolutely! I've heard so much about Melbourne's history and culture.

Well, you're in luck. Melbourne is rich in both. Did you know that it was once the capital of Australia?

Really? I had no idea.

Yes! Melbourne was the capital from 1901 to 1927 before it was moved to Canberra. Have you tried any Aussie food yet?

Yes, I had some kangaroo meat for dinner last night.

Oh, that's great! Australians love their meat. Have you heard of Vegemite?

Yes, I have, but I've never tried it before.

It's an acquired taste. Australians usually spread it on toast or crackers. Want to try it?

Sure, why not?

*hands Traveler B a piece of toast with Vegemite* Here you go. Do you like it?

*takes a bite* Hmm, it's definitely unique, but I don't hate it.

That's usually the reaction I get from first-timers. Moving on to some history, did you know that Melbourne was founded by John Batman?

Batman? Like the superhero?

*laughs* No, not quite. He was an explorer who settled in Melbourne with a group of people in 1835. They bought the land from the local Indigenous Australians.

Interesting. What else can you tell me about Melbourne's history?

Well, Melbourne was also the site of the famous Eureka Rebellion, which was a defining moment in Australian democracy.

I've never heard of that before. What was it about?

Basically, it was a protest by gold miners who were tired of being oppressed by the government. They created a flag with the Southern Cross and fought for their rights. The rebellion failed, but it paved the way for future reforms.

Wow, I didn't know Melbourne had such a rich and rebellious history. Thanks for sharing that with me!

You're welcome! Melbourne has a lot of hidden gems that are just waiting to be discovered. I hope you enjoy the rest of your stay here.
