Sure! The Gardens have over 8,500 species of plants from around the world. They were first established in 1846 and have been a popular spot for locals and tourists alike ever since.

Federation Square is a cultural hub in the heart of the city. It’s home to the Ian Potter Centre: NGV Australia, the Australian Centre for the Moving Image, and many restaurants and bars.

The Melbourne Museum is home to a range of exhibitions about Australia’s history, including a dinosaur exhibit and a display about Indigenous culture.

The market has been operating since 1878 and is Melbourne’s largest open-air market. You can find everything from fresh produce to clothing and souvenirs there.
