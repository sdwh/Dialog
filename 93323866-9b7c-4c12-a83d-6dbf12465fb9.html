Good morning, class! Today, we're going to talk about idioms. Can anyone tell me what an idiom is?

Is it an expression or phrase that doesn't mean exactly what the words say?

Exactly! Very good, B. Can you give me an example?

Um, how about "kick the bucket"? It means to die, but it doesn't have anything to do with kicking a bucket.

Excellent! You're a natural. Now, let's see if you can match some idioms with their meanings. Who wants to go first?

I'll go! Let's see... "hold your horses." That means to wait, right?

That's correct! You're on a roll. Let's try another one. "Bite the bullet."

Hmm, I think that means to face a difficult situation bravely.

Very good! You've got a real knack for this. One last one. "Cutting corners."

That means to do something the cheapest or easiest way, even if it's not the best or most honest way.

Excellent job, B! I think I might have a future idiom expert in my classroom.
