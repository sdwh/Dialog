Hello, how can I assist you today?

Hi there, I’ve been recently diagnosed with type 2 diabetes and I’m a bit overwhelmed with all the medication options available.

No worries, let's go over your options. We have a few oral medications such as metformin and sulfonylureas that help lower blood sugar levels.

Great, what about insulin injections? I’ve heard those are pretty common for people with type 2 diabetes.

Yes, that is an option as well. However, it is usually reserved for when oral medications are not effective enough in controlling blood sugar levels.

I see, how often will I need to take these medications?

It will depend on your doctor's recommendations and your current blood sugar levels. Typically, oral medications are taken once or twice a day, and insulin injections may be taken multiple times a day.

That makes sense. Are there any lifestyle changes that can also help manage my diabetes?

Absolutely, making changes to your diet and getting regular exercise can greatly improve your blood sugar control. It's also important to monitor your blood sugar levels regularly and attend regular check-ups with your doctor.

Thank you for all of your help, I feel much better about managing my diabetes and understanding my treatment options!

You're welcome! Please don't hesitate to reach out if you have any further questions or concerns.
