Good afternoon Chef B! It's so exciting to be here at this Michelin-starred restaurant in Geneva.

Thank you, it's my pleasure to have you here for an exclusive interview.

Let's start with a basic question, what would you say is the distinguishing characteristic of Swiss cuisine?

Swiss cuisine is simple, fresh and varied, reflecting the country's regional differences and influences from its neighboring countries. We use local and seasonal ingredients and make sure every dish has a unique identity.

Sounds interesting! How do you incorporate local ingredients into your dishes?

At our restaurant, we heavily rely on fresh and organic produce from local farmers and markets. We find ways to combine traditional ingredients with modern cooking techniques.

Could you tell us more about the cooking techniques you employ to make the dishes more unique?

One technique I really like is sous-vide cooking, where we vacuum-seal the ingredients and cook them in a precisely controlled water bath. It helps to retain the natural flavors and nutrients of the food.

That sounds exciting. What's your favorite creation on the menu?

I love our truffle risotto, which is made with locally sourced black truffles and Arborio rice. The rich, creamy texture and aroma are heavenly!

Mmm, that sounds divine. What would you say is the key to making a great dish?

It's all about the attention to detail, balance of flavors, and keeping it simple. It's important to let the natural flavors of the ingredients shine.

I definitely agree. Finally, what advice would you give to aspiring chefs?

Keep experimenting and don't be afraid of trying new combinations. Most importantly, cook with love and passion, and you will always create something special.

That's such wise advice. Thank you so much for your time, Chef B. It was a pleasure speaking with you.

Thank you for the opportunity, and enjoy the rest of your meal!
